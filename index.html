Our Story

We had our first date in Charlottesville, Virginia at the Downtown Mall. It was obvious from the start that we enjoyed being in each other's company. After dinner at Citizen Burger Bar, we walked around the downtown area sharing stories. Neither of us wanted the date to end, so we stopped in at Splendora's Gelato for dessert. When we both got home we made plans to have a second date the next day.

Justin showed up with maple donuts and raspberry smoothies from his all-time favorite store Wegmans, and we drove to Richmond making plans along the way. We ended up having lunch at Maggiano's, walking around the Short Pump mall, and visiting a local park to walk around in nature. 4 years later we returned to the same park where Justin proposed!

We love to travel and visit new places. Our favorites have been visits to the cherry blossom festival in Washington, D.C., a trip down memory lane in Rochester, NY, a very windy visit to Niagara Falls, and a family trip to Dallas, TX.

Getting There

Transportation

The venue is located two miles east of Skyline Drive's Swift Run Gap entrance and 5 miles west of Stanardsville.

If you are traveling west on Route 33:
Follow US-33 W. and pass the town of Stanardsville. The road will narrow and you will climb some winding foothills. We are the next left after the Lydia Log Cabin building with the Red Tin Roof. Turn LEFT onto VA-625/GOOSE POND RD, then take an immediate right into our parking lot.

If you are traveling east on Route 33:
After passing the Swift Run Gap entrance to Skyline Drive, you will begin to descend the mountain. You will pass a sign stating "You are now leaving Shenandoah National Park." Take the next RIGHT onto VA-625/GOOSE POND RD, then take an immediate right into our parking lot.

Do not rely only on GPS, it can be unreliable in this area. Lost? Call 434-566-2555.

What is there to do in the area?

Visit Charlottesville has wonderful guides on things to do in the area. Charlottesville is known for it's historical sites, wineries/breweries, and local restaurants. There are a number of food & drink tours and history tours, detailed here. Below are a few of our favorite things about the area:
  • Carter Mountain Orchard: Family owned and operated orchard that welcomes visitors to enjoy pick-your-own fruit, homemade cider donuts, local gifts, and breathtaking views overlooking Charlottesville.
  • Shenandoah National Park: A land bursting with cascading waterfalls, spectacular vistas, fields of wildflowers, and quiet wooded hollows. Take a drive along Skyline Drive or hike the many trails or mountains.
  • Saunders-Monticello Trail: With native hardwood forest, deep ravines and spectacular views of the Blue Ridge Mountains, the trail winds its way toward Monticello and back in a very manageable four miles. A maximum grade of 5% keeps it completely accessible to walkers, cyclists and those in wheelchairs.
  • Charlottesville Historic Downtown Mall: A pedestrian mall located in historic downtown Charlottesville. There is a wonderful mix of restored and renovated buildings for visitors to enjoy shopping, dining and visiting along the brick-paved pedestrian area. Park in the Water Street or Market Street parking garage and bring your walking shoes!
  • Decades Arcade: Pinball machines, video games, arcade cabinets, and home consoles spanning from the 50s to the present, all on free play for a fixed cost.
  • Michie Tavern: Servers in period attire offer bountiful Southern Midday Fare. Get a taste of the 18th-century featuring a buffet of southern fried chicken, marinated baked chicken, hickory-smoked pork barbecue, stewed tomatoes, black-eyed peas, buttermilk biscuits and so much more. Lunch served daily, 11:30 - 3:00.
  • Monticello: The primary residence of Thomas Jefferson — author of the Declaration of Independence and the Statute of Virginia for Religious Freedom, third president of the United States, and founder of the University of Virginia.
  • Montpelier: A memorial to James Madison - founding father and fourth president of the United States - and the Enslaved Community, a museum of American history, and a center for constitutional education.
  • Highland: The estate of James Monroe - a founding father and fifth president of the United States.
